Visualizing Datasets: AI Engineer Talk (Fall 2023)

Hat tip to @SimonW for the annotation format.

  • Speaker's Background: Junior AI engineer with a focus on generative AI and language models.
  • Experience: Self-taught, hands-on experience with various generative AI tools.
  • Talk Objective: To present visualized datasets relevant to AI engineering.

  • Puffin Dataset: Small but potent, only 3,000 records. Known for efficacy in fine-tuning base models.

    • Visualization: Scatter plot shows topic clusters.
    • Additional Info: Provides word scores to analyze prevalent topics.

  • SkunkWorks MOE Dataset: Part of the open-source community's effort to compete with GPT-4.

    • Size: 1.5 million rows, but only 10% is visualized.
    • Customization: Tool allows for viewing all 1.5 million rows if desired.
    • Topic Clusters: Displays most-mentioned words in large topic clusters.

  • Open Hermes Dataset: Another dataset used to fine-tune top models.

    • Highlight: Showcases diversity in vocabulary, topics, and plot shapes.

  • General Takeaway: Datasets have unique characteristics that influence fine-tuning outcomes.

    • Visualization Tools: Multiple plugins for scatter plots allow for different views of clusters.

  • Individual Practitioners: Useful for understanding the characteristics of self-created datasets.

    • Specific Use-Case: Discovering surprising patterns or conversations in the data.

  • Organizations: Essential for those looking to fine-tune models on company-specific data.

    • Dataset Curation: Combining company data with high-performing open-source datasets.

  • Langchhain Agents: Agents that query documents and collect data can benefit from visualization.

  • Retrieval Agents (RAG): Visualization aids in comprehending the topical landscape of embedded documents.

  • Personal Interest: Like the speaker, if you're keen on understanding what makes certain datasets special.

    • Fine-Tuning: Build your own dataset for more targeted model training.

  • General Utility: Visualizing large datasets offers a powerful way to understand their makeup.

  • Topic Balance: Visualization helps identify if your dataset is skewed towards certain topics like math or logic.

  • Alternative to Traditional Methods: Moves beyond SQL, databases, and CSVs to offer a new perspective.

  • Data Privacy: Allows for keeping data local, useful for company or personal data.

  • Role-Playing Models: Method is suitable for exploring NSFW or other specialized datasets.

  • Limitations:

    • Data Size: Difficult to fit very large datasets into interactive graphs.
    • Non-Deterministic: Multiple runs may yield different topics and labels even with the same settings.

  • GPU Recommendations: Nvidia 3060 or better for gaming PCs.

    • AMD/Intel GPUs: Slower due to lack of CUDA cores.

  • Apple Computers: M1 or M2 chips are sufficient but will run on CPU.

    • GGML Dependency: Future optimizations expected.

  • Operating Systems:

    • Windows: Use WSL2 for compatibility.
    • Unix-based: Mac OS and Linux generally work without issues.

  • Google Colab: An alternative, albeit less private, option.

  • GitHub Repo: All steps and code snippets available in a public repository.

    • https://github.com/YoungPhlo/visualizing-datasets-ai-engineer-fall-2023

  • Included Resources:

    • Notebook with the tutorial steps.
    • Additional code snippets for implementation.

  • Insights from Creators: Visualization often reveals overlooked aspects of the dataset.
  • Presenting to Stakeholders: Effective for conveying complex data to managers, boards, or investors in an understandable manner.

  • Other Tools on Radar:

    • Bulk, Galactic, Lilac are gaining attention.
    • Gonk is a new entrant worth exploring.

  • Collaboration Opportunities:

    • Open to testing community-created tools.
    • Willing to assist in exploring specific datasets.

  • Extended Tutorial:

    • Open to helping resolve issues, especially related to GPU setup with WSL2.

  • Feedback Welcome:

    • Encourages constructive criticism for process improvement.
